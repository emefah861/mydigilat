About Digilat

We believe everyone deserves to understand their legal rights — regardless of income, background, or experience with the law.

Our mission

Making legal information accessible to everyone

DigiLAT exists to help people better understand legal information in a way that is clear, accessible, and easy to follow.

Many people encounter legal situations such as tenancy issues, workplace disputes, debt collection, or contract concerns but struggle to understand what the law says or where they stand. At the same time, legal support is often out of reach, whether due to cost or eligibility.

We believe access to understandable legal information shouldn't depend on income or background.

Why this matters

Why this matters

In England and Wales, nearly 42 million people were unable to access a community care legal aid provider in their local area last year. This means around 70% of the population had limited or no practical access to publicly funded legal support, unless they could afford private representation.

For many, this leaves a gap between facing a legal issue and understanding the information needed to navigate it.

How we help

What we do

DigiLAT is a free tool that provides plain-English legal information based on UK law.

We help users understand:

  • What relevant areas of law may apply to a situation
  • How similar issues are commonly understood in legal terms
  • What rights are generally associated with those areas
  • Common next steps people typically take in these situations (such as gathering documents, reviewing agreements, or seeking appropriate formal support)

Our focus is not advice or representation, but making legal information easier to access, understand, and use as a starting point.

Important

What we are not

DigiLAT is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice. The information we provide is general in nature and based on publicly available UK legislation and guidance. Using our service does not create a solicitor-client relationship.

If your situation is urgent, complex, or involves court proceedings, we strongly recommend seeking professional legal advice. We can help point you in the right direction through our network of pro bono solicitors and free legal aid services such as Citizens Advice.

Legal information, not legal advice

DigiLAT provides general legal information based on UK law. The content and responses provided are for informational purposes only and should not be relied upon as legal advice. Using this service does not create a solicitor-client relationship. If your matter is urgent, we will direct you to qualified solicitors, Citizens Advice, and other free or low-cost legal aid services. Responses are generated using AI.
